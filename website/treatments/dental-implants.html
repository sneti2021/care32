What are dental implants?

A dental implant is a small, biocompatible titanium post that takes the place of a missing tooth root. It is placed into the jaw bone, where it gradually fuses with the bone to become a rock-solid foundation. A custom-made crown is then fitted on top - and the result looks, feels and works just like a natural tooth.

Unlike a removable denture or a bridge that relies on neighbouring teeth, an implant stands on its own. It also does something nothing else can: it stimulates the jaw bone exactly the way a natural root does, preventing the slow bone loss that follows a missing tooth. That's why dentists worldwide consider implants the gold standard for replacing teeth.

Who needs dental implants?

  • A single missing tooth - one implant and crown fills the gap without touching the healthy teeth on either side
  • Multiple missing teeth - implant-supported bridges replace several teeth without a removable plate
  • Loose, uncomfortable dentures - a few implants can anchor your denture firmly so it never slips again
  • A full arch of missing teeth - full-mouth solutions like All-on-4® restore an entire jaw on just four implants

If you currently wear a removable plate and are tired of it moving while you eat, do read about our implant-retained overdentures and All-on-4® solutions - they combine the affordability of a denture with the stability of implants.

How we do it - step by step

01

Assessment & 3D planning

We examine your mouth, take digital images and map your jaw in 3D. Our implant planning software lets us choose the exact position, angle and size of your implant before we ever begin.

02

Implant placement

Under local anaesthesia, the titanium implant is placed precisely into the planned position. The procedure is comfortable and usually takes about an hour.

03

Healing & osseointegration

Over the next 3-4 months, the implant fuses with your jaw bone - a natural process called osseointegration. You carry on with life as normal while it happens.

04

Abutment

Once the implant is solid, a small connector called an abutment is attached. This is the piece your new tooth will sit on.

05

Final crown

A custom crown - shade-matched to your natural teeth - is fixed onto the abutment. Bite, smile, done.

Aftercare - protecting your investment

  • Eat soft foods for the first few days; mild soreness settles quickly with prescribed medication
  • Keep the area clean - gentle brushing from day one, as we'll show you
  • Avoid smoking during healing; it significantly slows osseointegration
  • Once restored, brush and floss your implant exactly like a natural tooth
  • Come in for routine check-ups so we can monitor the implant and the gum around it
