What are crowns & bridges?

A crown is a custom-made cap that covers a damaged or weakened tooth completely - restoring its shape, strength and appearance. We work with all the modern options: all-porcelain/ceramic, porcelain-fused-to-metal (PFM), zirconia, gold alloy and lithium disilicate (e.max), choosing the material that best suits each tooth.

A bridge replaces one or more missing teeth by anchoring artificial teeth to the teeth (or implants) on either side of the gap. Depending on your situation we may recommend a traditional bridge, a cantilever bridge, a Maryland (resin-bonded) bridge that needs minimal preparation, or an implant-supported bridge that doesn't touch your natural teeth at all.

Both are fixed restorations - they stay in your mouth, day and night, and once fitted you simply forget they're there.

When you need a crown or bridge

  • A tooth that's cracked, fractured or badly worn down
  • A tooth weakened by a very large filling with little natural structure left
  • After root canal treatment - a crown protects the treated tooth from fracture
  • A badly discoloured or misshapen tooth you'd like to look natural again
  • One or more missing teeth, where a bridge can close the gap
  • Covering a dental implant with its final, natural-looking tooth

How we do it - step by step

01

Prep & scan

Under local anaesthesia, the tooth is gently shaped to make room for the crown, then recorded with a digital scan and shade-matched to its neighbours.

02

Temporary crown

A comfortable temporary protects the prepared tooth - you leave the clinic looking and chewing normally.

03

Lab fabrication

Your crown or bridge is precision-crafted in the laboratory from the chosen material, matched to your scan and shade.

04

Fitting & bonding

We check the fit, contact points and bite, make any fine adjustments, and permanently bond the restoration in place.

Aftercare - making it last

  • Mild sensitivity for a few days after fitting is normal and settles on its own
  • Brush twice daily and floss around the crown - the gum line still needs care
  • With a bridge, clean under the artificial tooth daily using floss threaders or an interdental brush
  • Avoid cracking nuts, ice or bones directly on ceramic restorations
  • Keep your routine check-ups so we can monitor the fit and the tooth beneath
