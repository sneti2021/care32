Healthy gums, healthy smile

Your gums do quiet, essential work: they anchor your teeth, cushion every bite and protect the bone underneath. When plaque and tartar build up along the gum line, the gums become inflamed - and what starts as a little bleeding while brushing can slowly progress to receding gums, loose teeth and bone loss.

Here's the good news: gum disease is reversible when caught early. A professional cleaning and a few small changes at home are often all it takes. And even in advanced cases, modern periodontal treatment - from deep cleaning to regeneration surgery - can stop the disease and rebuild what's been lost. At Care32 in Kukatpally, we treat every stage under one roof.

Warning signs you shouldn't ignore

  • Bleeding while brushing or flossing
  • Persistent bad breath that doesn't go away
  • Red, swollen or tender gums
  • Receding gums - teeth looking longer than before
  • Loose or shifting teeth
  • Gaps appearing between teeth that weren't there before

Gum disease rarely hurts until it's advanced - which is exactly why these quiet signs matter. If you've noticed even one, a short check-up can tell you exactly where you stand.

From cleaning to surgery - our treatment ladder

01

Assessment & gum charting

We measure the pocket depth around every tooth and chart your gum health, so we know precisely which stage we're treating - and you see it too.

02

Scaling & root planing

A thorough deep cleaning removes plaque and hardened tartar above and below the gum line, then smooths the root surfaces so gums can reattach.

03

Re-evaluation

A few weeks later we re-measure your pockets. Most patients heal beautifully at this stage and simply move to maintenance.

04

Flap surgery & regeneration, if needed

For deep pockets that persist, pocket reduction (flap) surgery cleans where instruments can't reach. Where bone has been lost, guided tissue regeneration, bone grafting or soft tissue grafts help rebuild support.

05

Maintenance recalls

Regular professional cleanings at a personalised interval keep your gums stable for the long run - the step that protects everything else.

Where teeth have already been lost to gum disease, bone grafting also prepares the jaw for dental implants - so a healthy, complete smile stays within reach.

Cosmetic gum treatments

Sometimes gums are perfectly healthy - they just don't frame your smile the way you'd like. Too much gum on display, an uneven gum line, or darker pigmentation can all be refined with precise, comfortable cosmetic procedures:

  • Crown lengthening - exposes more of the natural tooth for a balanced, proportionate smile
  • Gum contouring - reshapes an uneven gum line and corrects a gummy smile
  • Gum depigmentation - gently lightens darker gum patches for a brighter, pinker frame

These pair beautifully with professional teeth whitening when you want the complete refresh.

Aftercare - keeping gums healthy for good

  • Mild tenderness or sensitivity for a day or two after deep cleaning is normal and settles on its own
  • After gum surgery, follow the prescribed medication and avoid hard or spicy food for a few days
  • Brush gently twice a day and clean between teeth with floss or interdental brushes
  • Avoid tobacco - it's the single biggest risk factor for gum disease returning
  • Keep your maintenance recall appointments; consistency is what keeps gums stable
