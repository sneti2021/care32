What is a root canal?

Root canal treatment (endodontic therapy) saves a tooth whose inner nerve - the pulp - has become infected or damaged by deep decay, a crack or an injury. Instead of removing the tooth, our specialist gently removes the infected pulp, disinfects the fine canals inside the root, and seals them with a biocompatible filling.

The result: the pain is gone, the infection is cleared, and you keep your natural tooth. With a protective crown, a root canal treated tooth can last a lifetime.

Signs you may need a root canal

  • Severe, persistent toothache - especially while chewing
  • Sensitivity to hot or cold that lingers after the trigger is gone
  • Darkening or discolouration of a single tooth
  • Swelling or tenderness in the gums near the painful tooth
  • A recurring pimple or abscess on the gum
  • Deep decay or a large old filling that has started to ache

If any of these sound familiar, don't wait - early treatment is simpler, more comfortable and protects the tooth.

How we do it - step by step

01

Examine & image

A clinical exam and digital X-ray (with far lower radiation than film) show us exactly how far the infection has spread.

02

Numb completely

Modern local anaesthesia ensures the area is fully numb before we begin - most patients feel nothing beyond a small pinch.

03

Clean & shape

Using rotary instruments, the infected pulp is removed and each canal is precisely cleaned, shaped and disinfected.

04

Fill & seal

The canals are filled with biocompatible material and sealed to prevent reinfection - usually all in the same visit.

05

Protect with a crown

A zirconia or e.max crown restores full strength so you can chew confidently for years to come.

Aftercare - protecting your investment

  • Avoid chewing on the treated side until your crown is placed
  • Mild tenderness for a day or two is normal; prescribed medication handles it easily
  • Brush and floss normally - the treated tooth still needs daily care
  • Get the protective crown placed promptly; it's what gives the tooth long-term strength
  • Visit us for routine check-ups so we can keep an eye on the healed tooth
