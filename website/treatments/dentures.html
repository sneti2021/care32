What are dentures?

Dentures are custom-made replacements for missing teeth - either a full set for a whole jaw, or a partial that fills the gaps between your remaining natural teeth. A good denture restores far more than your smile: it brings back comfortable chewing, clear speech, and the facial support that keeps cheeks and lips looking full.

Denture-making has changed enormously. At Care32 we've replaced the old trays of impression putty with digital scanning, design the denture in CAD/CAM software, match the shade by computer, and fabricate in high-strength acrylics - including 3D-printed workflows. The result is a fit and finish that older dentures simply couldn't achieve.

One of our patients, Vin, put it best after bringing his mother to us: "Took my mom as she lost a lot of her teeth... My mom now has new teeth that look 100% natural."

Types of dentures we offer

  • Complete dentures - a full set replacing all teeth in the upper or lower jaw
  • Partial dentures - removable or flexible, filling gaps while your natural teeth stay untouched
  • Immediate dentures - placed the same day teeth are removed, so you never go without a smile
  • Implant-retained overdentures - snap securely onto a few dental implants, ending slipping for good
  • All-on-4® / All-on-6® - a fixed, full-arch set of teeth supported on just four or six implants
  • Hybrid dentures - combining the lightness of a denture with the stability of fixed implant support

Not sure which suits you? That's exactly what the first consultation is for - we'll examine your gums, bone and remaining teeth and walk you through the realistic options.

How we craft yours - step by step

01

Consultation & digital scan

We examine your mouth and capture it with a digital scanner - no gagging trays of putty, just a quick, comfortable scan.

02

Design & shade matching

Your denture is designed in CAD/CAM software, with computerised shade matching to suit your gum colour, skin tone and any remaining teeth.

03

Try-in

You preview the denture before it's finished - we check the look, the bite and your speech, and adjust anything you'd like changed.

04

Final fit

The finished denture, fabricated in high-strength acrylic with precision attachments where needed, is fitted and fine-tuned.

05

Follow-up adjustments

Small pressure spots are normal in the first weeks. Quick adjustment visits make sure your denture settles in completely comfortably.

Aftercare - keeping your dentures at their best

  • Clean your denture daily with a soft brush and denture cleanser - not regular toothpaste, which is too abrasive
  • Soak it overnight in water or a denture solution so the acrylic never dries out
  • Remove your denture at night to let your gums rest and breathe
  • Handle it over a folded towel or basin of water - dentures don't bounce
  • Come in for regular relines; gums change shape over time and a quick reline restores the snug fit
